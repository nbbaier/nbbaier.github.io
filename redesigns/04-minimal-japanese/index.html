About 03

I'm a Chicago-based software engineer with a background in theoretical linguistics. My academic work focused on syntax, morphology, and typology—particularly the morphosyntax of Ā-dependencies across the world's languages.

Previously, I was a postdoctoral researcher at the University of British Columbia and McGill University. I've conducted fieldwork on Seereer and Noon in Senegal, and contributed to documentation projects for Karuk and Montana Salish.

Now I build tools and explore how computational thinking can illuminate language structure—and how linguistic theory can inform better software design.
